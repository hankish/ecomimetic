# Participate

<p class="lead">Ecomimetic design is not a closed doctrine — it is a field shaped by the people who practice it. Here is how to get involved.</p>

## Join the Community of Practice

The ecomimetic design community spans architects, ecologists, materials scientists, urban planners, educators, and makers. Monthly gatherings — both in-person and online — provide space to share work-in-progress, debate principles, and forge collaborations across disciplines.

To join the next gathering, add your name to the interest list and we will be in touch with details. Participation is open to anyone working at the intersection of design and living systems.

## Contribute to the Manual

The Manual is a living document, and its best contributions come from practitioners testing these ideas in the field. If you have developed a framework, documented a case study, or refined a principle through your own work, we welcome your contribution.

Submissions are reviewed by a small editorial group and integrated into the Manual through a process of collaborative editing. Credit and attribution are always given. Visit the [Manual](/manual/) to see what is already there and identify where your knowledge might fit.

## Sponsor & Steward

Ecomimetic Design is an independent, non-commercial initiative. Financial support from aligned organizations allows us to maintain this resource, fund field research, and host gatherings. If your organization is interested in becoming a steward of this work, reach out directly. Stewardship does not confer editorial control — it funds the independence that makes this work trustworthy.
