# Ecomimetic Design

<p class="lead">A framework for designing systems that learn from, integrate with, and regenerate the living ecosystems they inhabit.</p>

## What Is Ecomimetic Design?

Ecomimetic design goes beyond biomimicry. Where biomimicry borrows forms and strategies from nature, ecomimetic design seeks to embed designed systems *within* ecological processes — making them participants in, rather than observers of, the living world. A building is not merely inspired by a forest; it breathes with one.

This requires rethinking the role of the designer, the timescale of the intervention, and the metrics we use to measure success. Resilience, reciprocity, and regeneration replace efficiency, optimization, and permanence as core values.

## A Living Body of Knowledge

This site is the hub for ecomimetic design knowledge — a growing manual, a media library, and a community of practice. The work here is iterative and open. We expect it to evolve as our collective understanding deepens.

Explore the [Manual](/manual/) for structured principles and frameworks, visit [Media](/media/) for essays and visual resources, or find out how to [Participate](/participate/) in shaping this field alongside us.
