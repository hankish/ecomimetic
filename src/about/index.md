# About

<p class="lead">Ecomimetic Design is an independent research initiative exploring what it means to design with — and for — living systems.</p>

## The Project

This work began with a simple question: what would it look like if the built environment participated in ecological processes rather than displacing them? The answer turned out to be less a set of techniques and more a shift in posture — a different relationship between designer, material, site, and time.

Ecomimetic design draws on systems ecology, vernacular building traditions, regenerative agriculture, and contemporary materials science. It is less interested in novelty than in continuity — in finding design lineages that can be extended, rather than disrupted.

## Principles

Ecomimetic Design is guided by a few core commitments:

**Reciprocity.** Designed systems should give back to the ecological context they enter — in biomass, in water, in habitat, in time.

**Legibility.** The relationship between a designed system and its ecological context should be visible and understandable to the people who live with it.

**Patience.** Ecomimetic design operates on ecological timescales. The most important outcomes may not be measurable for decades.

**Openness.** This knowledge belongs to the commons. All material on this site is freely available and openly licensed.
